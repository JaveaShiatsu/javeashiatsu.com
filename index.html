Philosophy

Zen Shiatsu is a traditional Japanese healing method that uses gentle pressure to release blockages and restore the body's natural flow of energy — known as Ki.

Work focuses along the body's meridian channels, the pathways through which this energy moves. Though Shiatsu literally means 'finger pressure', its true essence is communication through touch.

The result is deep relaxation, mental clarity, reduced stress, and relief from pain and tension — alongside improved circulation and the body's own capacity to restore itself.

Treatment

What to Expect from a Shiatsu Treatment

Rooted in the same principles as Chinese Acupuncture and Traditional Chinese Medicine, Shiatsu is a gentle, non-invasive therapy that works with the body's natural energy rather than against it.

Sessions are conducted fully clothed — you simply wear something light and comfortable, and lie on a futon on the floor. If needed, the treatment can easily be adapted to a chair or massage table.

Pressure is applied using thumbs, fingers, elbows, forearms and feet, combined with gentle stretches drawing on both Western physiotherapy and Oriental yoga traditions.

No undressing, no oils, no elaborate preparation — Shiatsu fits naturally into any setting or time of day.
